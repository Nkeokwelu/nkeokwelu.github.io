Comparing ESN and XGB for GB DA EPF without exogenous inputs

Data

  • Source: Nord Pool (Hourly prices, 2020–2024).
  • Preprocessed for daylight saving adjustments.

Approach

  • Time series analysis & decomposition.
  • Supervised dataset: previous day/week → next day.
  • Models: ESN (Echo State Network), XGBoost, Naive baseline.
  • Hyperparameter tuning via Bayesian Optimization for ESN.

Key Insights (1-Year Test Data)

  1. ESN outperforms both XGBoost and Naive models, achieving the lowest RMSE (£16.13) and MAPE (24.94%).
  2. ESN effectively captures non-linear dynamics and short-term variations in GB DA electricity prices.
  3. Naive performs reasonably but is less sophisticated, while XGBoost is competitive but slightly behind ESN.

Conclusion

ESN is the most effective model for GB DA EPF without exogenous inputs, providing a strong benchmark for GB DA EPF.

GB Power Mix Evolution Dashboard

Interactive Tableau visualization showing the evolution of Great Britain's power sector over 14+ years. This dashboard explores the UK's ongoing energy transition: the steady decline in fossil fuel reliance (from dominant in 2010 to 32% in 2023), the rise of renewables (led by wind at 28.9% in 2023), stable nuclear contributions (14.5%), growing imports (12.4%), and a dramatic drop in average carbon intensity — from peaks over 500 gCO₂/kWh down to 149 gCO₂/kWh in 2023. [Data Source: NESO].
